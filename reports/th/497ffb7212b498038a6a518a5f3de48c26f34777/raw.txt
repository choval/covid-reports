HIGHLIGHTS
• On the 23rd of April 2020, 13 new cases of
laboratory-confirmed COVID-19 were announced
by the Ministry of Public Health of Thailand,
bringing the total number of cases to 2,839.
• Of these, 85.6% (2,430) have recovered, about
1.7% (50) have died, and 12.6% (359) are receiving
treatment.
• Only one case was identified in a person returning
from outside Thailand; likely reflecting restrictions
on entry.
• Five of the new cases are close contacts of
previously confirmed cases. Two cases attended
crowded places.
• To date, 68 of the total 77 provinces have reported
cases. 35 of these 68 provinces have not reported
any new cases during the past 14 days. The three
provinces that have reported the most cases are
Bangkok (1,454), Phuket (197) and Nonthaburi
(152). Nine provinces have not reported any
cases.
UPDATE FROM THE MINISTRY

THAILAND:
Active case finding in Bangkok:
• From March 6th to 17th April at the Acute
Respiratory Infection outpatient clinic of the
Bangkok Urban Institute for Disease Prevention
and Control, a total of 3,350 samples were tested
and 94 (2.8%) were found to be positive.
• From April 15-22nd, active case finding was
conducted in the Bangkhen community of
Bangkok. The samples were taken from the
general population including asymptomatic and
asymptomatic street food vendors, garbage
pickup staff, taxi driver, motorcycle taxi driver,
delivery person and bus driver. A total of 1,876
samples were tested and one was found to be
positive. Next week active case finding will be
performed at the Klongtoei Districts

What who is doing to provide support to Thailand
WHO Thailand is in frequent direct contact with the
Royal Thai Government through the Ministry of Public
Health, sharing information on key developments,
guidelines and scientific updates.
WHO supports the wider UN response, including
working with key partners and the Ministry of Public
Health to support migrant populations in Thailand.
WHO also provides information and advice to staff of
the UN system in Thailand.
