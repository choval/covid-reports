Analysis of data of COVID-19 reported cases aged
0-14 years old
• Data from January – 26 April 2020 shows a total of
88 confirmed cases aged 0-14 years old (3% of total
confirmed cases).
• No fatalities reported among these cases.
Male: Female ratio = 1:1
• Nationality: Thai 90% and non-Thai 10%
• All three age groups have same level of risk of
infection: 0-4 years = 34%; 5-9 years = 32% and 10-14
years = 34%
• Most of them had close contact with previous
confirmed cases (86.4%), followed by travel history
abroad (3.4%), visiting crowded places (3.4%) and
no data (6.8%). Among the close contact infections,
major sources are from parents (45%) followed
by family members (24%), relatives (8%) and
unspecified (24%).

What WHO is doing to provide support to Thailand
WHO Thailand is in frequent direct contact with the Royal Thai
Government through the Ministry of Public Health, sharing
information on key developments, guidelines and scientific
updates.
WHO supports the wider UN response, including working with
key partners and the Ministry of Public Health to support migrant
populations in Thailand. WHO also provides information and
advice to staff of the UN system in Thailand.
Media
WHO Thailand’s website, Twitter and Facebook accounts regularly
provide content on all aspects of the outbreak in Thailand
and around the world. Media queries can be directed to

ADVICE FOR THE PUBLIC
UPDATE FROM THE THAI GOVERNMENT
• While the upcoming public holidays (1st, 4th,
6th and 11th May) are not cancelled, people are
requested to refrain from traveling across the
provinces for leisure.
UPDATE FROM THE BANGKOK INFECTIOUS
DISEASE PREVENTION AND CONTROL
COMMITTEE
• On 29th
April the Bangkok Metropolitan
Administration (BMA) announced 4 key measures:
1. Continued closure of high-risk establishments
(such as bars) until 31 May 2020 (in accordance with
emergency decree)
o Easing measures for 8 kinds of establishments
subject to compliance with set measures.
o The dates for these reopenings will be announced
later by CCSA
2. The eight types of establishment that can be
reopened under while complying strictly with
mandatory measures include:
o Restaurants (except those inside malls/
supermarkets)
o Market and flea markets
o Barbers and hair salons
o Clinics and health facilities (beauty clinics excluded)
o Golf courses and driving ranges
o Sport and exercise facilities – only sports that
not require close contact (running, tennis and
badminton)
o Pet grooming shops
o Parks - no use of exercise equipment or mass
gatherings.
3. Non-compliance with mandatory measures will be a
violation of the law. Penalties to be announced.
4. A decision on the sale of alcohol will follow the CCSA
announcement.
